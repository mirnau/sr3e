## Spellcasting Preparation

- Casting a spell is declared as a Complex Action.
- The caster chooses the spell to cast.
- The caster chooses the spell Force.
- The chosen Force cannot exceed the caster's learned maximum for that spell.
- The caster chooses how many Sorcery dice to use.
- The caster chooses how many Spell Pool dice to use.
- The caster can cast multiple spells simultaneously by splitting dice among them.
- Each additional simultaneous spell adds +2 to the Drain Resistance Test.

## Spellcasting Sequence

- The spellcasting sequence resolves within the spellcasting Complex Action.
- The spellcasting sequence consists of preparation, targeting, Sorcery Test, Resistance Test, spell effect, and Drain Resistance.

## Spell Targeting

- The caster must have clear line of sight to the target.
- For touch-range spells, the caster must physically touch the target.
